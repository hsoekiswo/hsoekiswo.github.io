Supraspinatus Sardines, bee pollen, spirulina, propolis, raw honey, water
Teres major Whole protein foods (meat, fish, eggs, dairy products)
Pectoralis major clavicular Vitamin B as found in wheat germ, whole grains, liver, brewers yeast
Latissimus dorsi Vitamin A as found in yellow and green leafy vegetables. Avoid refined sugars and other concentrated sweets.
Subscapularis Foods which contain calcium, Vitamin E and B, e.g. brewers yeast, wheat germ, bone, dairy products, egg yolk, green leafy vegetables, peas, beans, nuts, strawberries, lemons and oranges
Quadriceps Vitamin B as found in yeast, wheat germ, yogurt, buttermilk, liver
Peroneus Foods rich in thiamine (B1) such as peas, beans, wheat germ, whole grains, raw fruit, cabbage, egg yolk, yeast. Also calcium foods such as bone, dairy products, egg yolk, green leafy vegetables, beans, nuts. Avoid foods contain- ing oxalic acid (cranberries, coffee, chocolate, purple fruits) at meals when calcium is consumed.
Psoas Water (preferably filtered), kidney meat, parsley, green pepper, green leafy vegetables, wheat germ and green peas. Avoid coffee and tea.
Gluteus medius Foods rich in Vitamin E (wheat germ, green peas, green leafy vegetables)
Teres minor Organic iodine found in seaweed (kelp) and raw or slightly cooked fish
Anterior deltoid Avoid fats and grilled or fried foods. Eat non fat food sources of Vitamin A such as carrots, parsley and other yellow and green vegetables.
Pectoralis major sternal Avoid fried foods and sweets which contain fats, alcoholic beverages, carbonated drinks and caffeine. Liver and foods containing Vitamin A (green leafy vegetables, parsley, green pepper) may be helpful.
Anterior serratus Vitamin C foods such as green peppers, citrus, and increased water.
Fascia lata Increased fibre and water intake, yoghurt, buttermilk; honey and increased carbohydrates may help temporarily if constipated, but avoid with diarrhoea. Persistent bilateral weakness may be helped by eating liver, brewers yeast and green leafy vegetables.
